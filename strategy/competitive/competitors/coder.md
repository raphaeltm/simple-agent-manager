# Competitor Profile: Coder

**Last Updated**: 2026-03-11
**Category**: Direct
**Website**: https://coder.com

## Overview
Coder provides self-hosted cloud development environments with a strong emphasis on enterprise AI agent governance. They provision governed, self-hosted environments for developers and approved coding agents on enterprise infrastructure. Open-source core (Community tier) with enterprise Premium tier.

## Target Market
- Large enterprises (financial services, government, defense)
- Organizations with strict compliance requirements (GDPR, DORA, SOC 2)
- Platform engineering teams using Terraform
- Companies with air-gapped or on-premises requirements

## Product
- Self-hosted CDEs on any infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Azure, Kubernetes, Docker, OpenShift)
- Terraform-based infrastructure-as-code for environment definitions
- **Coder Workspaces**: Self-hosted development environments
- **Coder Mux**: Browser/desktop app for running parallel coding agents
- Multi-IDE support: VS Code, JetBrains, Cursor, Jupyter
- AI agent governance with permissions, audit logging, compliance controls

## Pricing
- **Community**: Free, open source
- **Premium**: Contact sales (no public pricing)
- 30-day trial available
- Listed on AWS Marketplace

## Strengths
- Enterprise trust and compliance (SOC 2, GDPR, air-gapped support)
- Self-hosted on customer infrastructure — appeals to security-conscious orgs
- Terraform-based — familiar to platform engineering teams
- Multi-IDE support including Cursor
- Open-source core builds community and trust
- AI agent governance is a timely differentiator

## Weaknesses
- No public pricing — friction for individual developers and small teams
- Requires Terraform expertise for setup
- Self-hosted means operational burden on the customer
- No BYOC model for individual cloud accounts — more "bring your own infrastructure"
- Enterprise sales cycle (demo required)

## Recent Moves (as of 2026-03-11)
- Added AI agent governance features (permissions, audit trails, compliance)
- Launched Coder Mux for parallel AI agent execution
- Recognized by Gartner in CDE category

## Strategic Direction
Moving upmarket into enterprise AI governance. Positioning as "the secure way to run AI coding agents at scale" rather than just a CDE platform.

## Key Differentiators vs SAM
- **Coder advantage**: Enterprise compliance, self-hosted on any infra, multi-IDE support, Terraform ecosystem
- **SAM advantage**: BYOC model (simpler than self-hosting), serverless control plane (no infra to manage), chat-first UX, lower barrier to entry
